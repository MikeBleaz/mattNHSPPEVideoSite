Does the ward you work in have a safe corridor?


If you are not sure whether your ward has a safe corridor, please speak to your supervisor.


Safe corridor: To be a safe corridor, it must have the following:
  • A corridor that connects all the patient rooms and can be closed to patients.
  • All the single occupancy or multi-occupancy rooms must have an en-suite toilet and shower. So, patients do not need to use the corridor to get to the toilet or shower.
  • The single occupancy or multi-occupancy rooms must be separated from the corridor by a door.
